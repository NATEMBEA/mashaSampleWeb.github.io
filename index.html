Dr. Tsofa

Associate Professor. Health Policy and Systems Specialist

Prof. Benjamin Tsofa is a Senior Principal Research Scientist, Health Policy & Systems Research, and former Centre Director of the KEMRI – Centre for Geographic Medicine Research – Coast. He holds a Bachelor of Dental Surgery degree from the University of Nairobi; and and a PGD, MSc and DrPH from the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine. His research work focuses on applying systems thinking approaches and theories in examining various aspects of the organisation and functioning of health systems, including on health system governance, health financing, human resources for health, and service delivery systems; to tackle current (and future) health system challenges - focusing on national, sub-national, service delivery (facility) and community levels in Kenya and across various countries in the African region. He also works on applying various policy analysis and political economy theories in examining health policy design and implementation; and on policy engagement and knowledge translation for supporting evidence use in health system decision making. He currently leads the Health Policy and Systems Research Group, at the KEMRI-Wellcome Trust Research Programme; and is Head of the Health Systems PhD Training Program at the KEMRI Graduate School. He holds various Visiting and/or Adjunct Associate Professorship positions including, at the KEMRI Graduate School - Nairobi, Pwani University – Kilifi, and Strathmore University - Nariobi, and a visiting Teaching Fellow at Oxford University. He serves in several Technical Advisory Committees and Technical Working Groups, at the National Ministry of Health in Kenya, to support to various health system strengthening initiatives; and across other countries in the region, with the WHO Regional Office for Africa (WHO-AFRO)

University of London Doctor of Public Health, Public Health Systems and Policy
University of London MSc in Public Health, Health Systems and Health Policy

Dr. Chapaa

Research Scientist with interests in impacts of climate change on public health.

As a Research Scientist and Project Manager at Emory University, Moses leads and coordinates research projects that aim to understand and reduce the transmission of infectious diseases in different settings and populations. He has more than 14 years of international experience in public health, data analysis, and scientific communication, and holds a PhD in Public Health of Infectious Diseases from The Open University. Moses is passionate about using empirical data to guide the design and execution of programs that offer solutions to challenges in human health, sanitation, and poverty. He is currently involved in multi-country projects that characterize social contact patterns in workplaces, schools, and households in the USA, Mozambique, Guatemala, India, and Pakistan. These data will be useful to inform and evaluate new and better interventions against disease spread. He also has experience in teaching infectious disease dynamics at the postgraduate level and is an active member of the Rotary Club of Kilifi.

The Open University The Open University Doctor of Philosophy (PhD), Social Networks and Epidemiology of Infectious Diseases
Doctor of Philosophy (PhD), Social Networks and Epidemiology of Infectious Diseases
